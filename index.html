Problem Statement

Behavioral health program operations lacked centralized scheduling, compliance tracking, and KPI reporting. Scheduling conflicts occurred in 28% of planned sessions, documentation compliance sat at 94%, and leadership reporting required 3–4 hours of manual work weekly.

Goal Statement

Reduce scheduling conflict rate by ≥15 percentage points, achieve 100% documentation compliance, increase monthly admissions by ≥10%, and reduce weekly reporting time by ≥50% — within a 6-month implementation window using Lean Six Sigma DMAIC.

Solutions Implemented

1
Power BI Operations Dashboard
Built centralized Power BI dashboard consolidating scheduling efficiency, compliance rates, engagement trends, staff utilization, and admissions data. Reduced manual reporting from 3–4 hours to under 30 minutes.
2
Standardized Meeting Cadence & Dependency Framework
Implemented structured weekly meetings across departments with pre-defined agenda templates, dependency tracking, and 48-hour conflict resolution protocols. Reduced scheduling conflicts from 28% to 13%.
3
EMR Documentation Protocol
Created standardized checklists, real-time audit triggers, and weekly compliance reviews integrated into staff workflows. Achieved 100% documentation compliance within 60 days.
4
Evidence-Based Curriculum Standardization (3 Tracks)
Applied DMAIC to develop standardized curricula across three treatment tracks, reducing process variation and enabling consistent, measurable outcomes. Contributed to 15% admissions increase.
5
Cross-Department Communication Framework
Established documented handoff protocols, RACI matrix, and stakeholder communication standards — eliminating ambiguity in role ownership and escalation paths.
