Project 01

Integrative Recovery Program Design and Launch

Mountain Springs Recovery   Early 2022 to late 2023

Program Design Six Sigma Cross-functional Leadership KPI Development Budget Management Change Management

There was no defined curriculum, performance metrics, documentation framework, and no process built yet for staff training or quality review. The goal of implementing this program was to provide a portfolio that delivered top end therapuetic services. The result was a fully operational, evidence-based program serving 30 to 40 clients with measurable outcomes tracked from the start.

The Problem

Building infrastrucutre from the ground up.

The organization needed a standardized service delivery program. This program provided a defined scope, scheduling framework, performance visibility, structured onboarding process for the staff responsible for delivering services to clients in a high-stakes environment.

My Approach

End-to-end programs built using Lean Six Sigma methodology.

Scoped the program, defined deliverables, and built a scheduling and documentation framework. Applied Six Sigma and evidence-based frameworks to design standardized curricula. Coordinated teams across clinical, operations, and administrative departments, managing dependencies and delivery risk throughout. Built KPI dashboards for leadership reporting. Trained and supervised 5 to 10 staff members with regular performance review cycles.

The Outcome

Measurable growth in admissions, retention, and compliance.

Program implementation drove a 20% increase in admissions and extended average client retention and engagement by one full month, enabling significantly better treatment outcomes. Continuous improvement feedback loops improved year-over-year participation and satisfaction. Zero compliance findings throughout the program's operation.

Project 02

Virtual Program Launch and COVID-19 Services Manual

Long-Term Care Setting   2020 to 2021

Crisis Response Remote Program Design Technical Writing Stakeholder Communication Inclusive Design Collaborative Delivery

When COVID-19 forced full nursing home lockdowns, 20 resident faced complete social isolation with no virtual programming infrastructure and no adaptive protocols for remote delivery. Working with a two-person team, a fully virtual program was designed, launched, and documented under crisis conditions. The result was a 20% improvement in reported overall wellbeing and a reusable manual distributed to staff and families alike.

The Problem

Pandemic isolation with zero remote infrastructure.

A sudden complete lockdown left residents with no programming, no family connection, and no structure for their days. There was no existing playbook for virtual delivery in a long-term care setting and no resources for families trying to support engagement from outside the facility.

My Approach

Designed a virtual program and co-authored a standardized manual.

Collaborated with a two-person team to design and launch a virtual programming model, applying evidence-based practices adapted across diverse cognitive and physical ability levels. Co-developed the COVID-19 Recreation Services Manual, a standardized reference document distributed to staff and resident families. Applied iterative, data-driven improvements throughout to increase participation and inclusivity.

The Outcome

20% wellbeing improvement. A reusable, scalable playbook.

Measured a 20% improvement in participants' reported overall wellbeing during mandatory isolation. The standardized manual became a reusable resource beyond the original scope, distributable to any long-term care facility navigating similar circumstances, extending the project's value well past its original timeline.
