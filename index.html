Problem Statement

Behavioral health program operations at Mountain Springs Recovery lacked centralized scheduling, compliance tracking, and KPI reporting infrastructure. Scheduling conflicts occurred in 28% of planned sessions, documentation compliance sat at 94%, and leadership reporting required 3–4 hours of manual work weekly. No data-driven decision-making framework existed.

Goal Statement

Reduce scheduling conflict rate by ≥15 percentage points, achieve 100% documentation compliance, increase monthly admissions by ≥10%, and reduce weekly reporting time by ≥50% — within a 6-month implementation window using Lean Six Sigma DMAIC methodology.

Solutions Implemented

1
Power BI Operations Dashboard
Built a centralized Power BI dashboard consolidating scheduling efficiency, compliance rates, engagement trends, staff utilization, and admissions data into a single weekly executive reporting view. Reduced manual reporting from 3–4 hours to under 30 minutes.
2
Standardized Meeting Cadence & Dependency Framework
Implemented a structured weekly meeting schedule across clinical, operations, and administrative departments with pre-defined agenda templates, dependency tracking, and 48-hour conflict resolution protocols. Reduced scheduling conflicts from 28% to 13%.
3
EMR Documentation Protocol
Created standardized documentation checklists, real-time audit triggers, and weekly compliance reviews integrated into staff workflows. Achieved 100% documentation compliance within 60 days of implementation.
4
Evidence-Based Curriculum Standardization (3 Tracks)
Applied Six Sigma DMAIC to develop standardized curricula across three treatment tracks, reducing process variation and enabling consistent, measurable client outcomes. Contributed to 15% admissions increase and 30-day retention improvement.
5
Cross-Department Communication Framework
Established documented handoff protocols, RACI matrix, and stakeholder communication standards across clinical, operations, and administrative departments — eliminating ambiguity in role ownership and escalation paths.
